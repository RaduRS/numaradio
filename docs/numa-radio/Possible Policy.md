AI Radio Policy and Terms

  
Overview
  

This document sets out a practical policy for an AI music radio service that broadcasts AI-generated songs, accepts listener prompts, uses synthetic host voice segments, and allows broad downstream reuse of songs by listeners. The policy is written in plain English so it can be adapted into a public-facing Terms, FAQ, or rights page.

The service model described here is compatible with the current direction of AI-first radio and AI music streaming products, including examples of AI-created radio concepts and social AI music platforms where users can stream, remix, or build on generated tracks.

  

Core product policy

  

The service broadcasts two types of music:

- Catalog tracks previously generated by the operator using approved AI music tools.
- On-demand tracks generated from live listener prompts.

Synthetic spoken segments, such as station IDs, intros, weather, queue updates, and shout-outs, may be generated using a text-to-speech provider. Users submit prompts with the understanding that the service may transform, shorten, moderate, or reject prompts before generation.

  

Rights position

  

Songs created on Suno while the creator was on a paid subscription are described by Suno as eligible for commercial use by that paying creator.[cite:31][cite:33] Suno has also stated that ownership and commercial use do not automatically mean that a song is fully protected by copyright in every jurisdiction, especially where a track is entirely AI-generated.[cite:25][cite:31]

Because of that distinction, this service should avoid claiming that every broadcast song is protected by exclusive copyright. A safer and clearer statement is that the service has permission to use and broadcast the songs it uploads, but listeners should assume reuse rights come from the service's own permission policy rather than guaranteed copyright exclusivity.[cite:25][cite:31]

  

Recommended public listener terms

  

The following plain-English listener terms are suitable for a permissive-reuse product:

1. Songs played on the service may be recorded, downloaded, clipped, reused, reposted, remixed, or synchronized by listeners unless a specific track page says otherwise.
2. The service gives listeners permission to reuse songs obtained from the station at their own risk.
3. The service does not guarantee that any given AI-generated song qualifies for copyright protection in every country.
4. The service may remove, replace, or reclassify tracks later if a rights issue, platform-policy issue, or third-party claim appears.
5. Listeners must not falsely claim endorsement, trademark ownership, or authorship of station branding, host voices, or other service identifiers.

This approach matches the product's stated goal of broad reuse while staying aligned with the reality that AI music rights can be commercially usable without being cleanly enforceable as exclusive copyright.[cite:25][cite:31][cite:33]

  

Recommended creator and upload terms

  

If the operator uploads Suno-made songs or songs from other generators, the service should adopt these creator-side rules:

- Upload only songs created during a subscription tier or license state that allows commercial use.[cite:31][cite:33]
- Keep internal provenance records for each track, including tool used, account tier, creation date, prompt, and exported file reference.
- Do not upload songs that intentionally imitate named artists, use unlicensed vocals, or include third-party copyrighted material.
- Treat songs from different generators separately because one provider's commercial policy does not automatically apply to another provider's output.

This matters because Suno's public help guidance has changed over time, and outside reporting has highlighted the gap between platform-granted commercial use and broader copyright certainty.[cite:25][cite:31][cite:33]

  

Voice and announcement policy

  

All synthetic DJ voices, intros, weather reads, and announcements should be labeled as AI-generated or synthetic where practical. That keeps the service transparent and reduces the chance of users assuming a real human presenter or a licensed celebrity voice is involved.

Prompted shout-outs and spoken inserts should pass through moderation before generation or playback. The service should block impersonation, defamation, slurs, explicit sexual content involving minors, threats, private personal data, and requests that misuse public figures or brands.

  

Moderation policy

  

The service may reject or rewrite prompts that:

- Target a real artist in a way that implies imitation or cloning.
- Use hateful, abusive, or illegal content.
- Include private personal information.
- Infringe trademarks or misrepresent sponsorship.
- Appear designed to generate takedown bait or rights conflicts.

A permissive reuse model works best when generation rules are stricter at the prompt layer. That keeps the catalog easier to defend even if downstream listener reuse is intentionally loose.

  

Station disclaimer language

  

The following public disclaimer is suitable for the site footer or upload page:

Songs on this station may be AI-generated and may not be eligible for copyright protection in all jurisdictions. Where the station has the right to upload and broadcast a track, the station may also choose to let listeners reuse that track. Reuse is allowed by station policy unless a track says otherwise, but users remain responsible for checking whether their use complies with platform rules, local law, and third-party rights.

  

Operational checklist

  

Before launch, the service should:

- Create a provenance log for every uploaded song.
- Separate catalog songs from live-generated songs in internal records.
- Add a track-level status field such as broadcast only, listener reuse allowed, or restricted.
- Publish a visible AI disclosure for music and voice segments.
- Publish a takedown contact and response policy.
- Review each generator's commercial terms before enabling it in production.

  

Product positioning

  

The market already includes AI-created radio concepts and AI music platforms with community listening and reuse dynamics, but the combination of live requests, instant generated songs, synthetic host segments, and openly permissive listener reuse still appears differentiated.[cite:9][cite:44] That makes the clearest positioning: a request-driven AI station with explicit reuse permissions and transparent synthetic hosting.
